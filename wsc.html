Williams - Sonoma Cooks

Opportunity

How can Williams-Sonoma improve customers' experiences beyond the products they buy?

Solution

A web experience that allows users to browse and book cooking classes

Role

This was a solo project. My design process included research, planning, design and iteration.



Process

Over the course of two weeks I...

  • Analyzed Williams-Sonoma and its competitors (specifically those with cooking classes)
  • Researched home cooks' habits and goals
  • Discovered what information users need when choosing cooking classes
  • Simplified the global navigation based on what users wanted
  • Conducted three rounds of user testing

    • Results & Learning

    The prototype was visually pleasing to users. Through mulitple iterations, I was able to design a navigation that was intuitive for users. Usability testing was an extremely valuable process; I going through rapid iterations and observing how much use improved between versions.

    Within this project, I experienced the difference between designing for the user and designing for the client. One of the features I was tasked with - the interactive shopping list - was very difficult for me to create because none of my users understood the function (because they didn't need it). On the other hand, I felt more confident designing the class-booking process because I had many substantiated insights on what users wanted.
